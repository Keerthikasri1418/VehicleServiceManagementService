Tips for Two Wheelers

      • Check Tire Pressure:
        • Regularly check and maintain the correct tire pressure. Proper inflation improves fuel efficiency and handling.
      • Inspect Brakes:
        • Check brake pads and discs for wear. Replace pads if they are thin, and ensure the brake fluid is at the recommended level.
      • Oil Changes:
        • Follow the recommended oil change intervals. Use the right type and grade of oil specified in the manual.
      • Chain Care:
        • Keep the chain lubricated and properly tensioned. Clean it regularly and replace if it shows signs of wear.
      • Inspect Lights and Indicators:
        • Regularly check that all lights, including headlights, brake lights, and turn signals, are working properly.
      • Fluid Levels:
        • Check and maintain the levels of essential fluids, including engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and transmission fluid.
      • Clean Air Filter:
        • Clean or replace the air filter according to the maintenance schedule. A clean filter improves engine performance and fuel efficiency.
      • Inspect Suspension:
        • Check the suspension components for leaks and wear. Ensure proper functioning for a smoother ride.
      • Battery Check:
        • Inspect the battery for corrosion, and ensure tight connections. Check the water level in non-sealed batteries if applicable.
      • Inspect Cables and Controls:
        • Regularly check throttle, clutch, and brake cables for proper operation. Lubricate if necessary.
      • Rust Prevention:
        • Apply a rust inhibitor or protective coating to metal parts, especially in areas prone to corrosion.
      • Check for Leaks:
        • Look for any oil, coolant, or fuel leaks. Address any leaks promptly to prevent damage and ensure safety.
      • Keep it Clean:
        • Regularly clean your two-wheeler to remove dirt, grime, and corrosive substances. Pay attention to hard-to-reach areas.
      • Inspect Spark Plug:
        • Check and replace the spark plug as recommended by the manufacturer. A clean spark plug promotes efficient combustion.
      • Test Horn and Mirrors:
        • Ensure that the horn is functioning properly, and adjust mirrors for optimal visibility.
      • Maintain Proper Riding Gear:
        • Regularly inspect and maintain your riding gear, including helmets, gloves, and protective clothing.
      • Periodic Professional Inspection:
        • Schedule periodic inspections by a qualified mechanic, especially for tasks like valve adjustments and other specialized checks.
      • Store Properly:
        • If storing the bike for an extended period, use a bike cover and consider lifting the tires off the ground.
      • Keep a Toolkit:
        • Carry a basic toolkit on your rides for minor adjustments and repairs.


Tips for Four Wheelers


      • Regular Oil Changes:
        • Follow the manufacturer's recommendations for oil changes. Regular oil changes keep the engine lubricated and running smoothly.
      • Check Tire Pressure:
        • Maintain proper tire pressure to ensure better fuel efficiency, handling, and tire longevity. Check tire pressure regularly, including the spare tire.
      • Rotate Tires:
        • Rotate your tires regularly to promote even wear. This extends the life of your tires and improves overall vehicle performance.
      • Brake System Inspection:
        • Regularly check the brake pads, discs, and fluid levels. Replace worn brake pads promptly to maintain effective braking.
      • Fluid Checks:
        • Check and top up all essential fluids, including coolant, brake fluid, transmission fluid, and power steering fluid.
      • Battery Maintenance:
        • Clean the battery terminals to prevent corrosion. Check the battery voltage and replace it if needed.
      • Air Filter Replacement:
        • Regularly inspect and replace the air filter to ensure proper air intake, fuel efficiency, and engine performance.
      • Alignment and Suspension Checks:
        • Periodically check the wheel alignment and inspect the suspension system for any signs of wear or damage.
      • Inspect Belts and Hoses:
        • Check the condition of belts and hoses for signs of wear, cracks, or leaks. Replace them if necessary.
      • Lights and Indicators:
        • Regularly check all lights, including headlights, brake lights, turn signals, and interior lights. Replace bulbs as needed.
      • Windshield Wipers:
        • Replace windshield wipers regularly, especially if they show signs of wear or are not effectively clearing the windshield.
      • Keep it Clean:
        • Regularly wash and wax your vehicle to protect the paint and finish. Clean the interior to maintain a comfortable and pleasant driving environment.
      • Rust Prevention:
        • Apply a rust inhibitor or corrosion-resistant spray to vulnerable areas, especially in regions with harsh weather conditions.
      • Check for Leaks:
        • Periodically inspect the vehicle for any fluid leaks. Address leaks promptly to prevent damage and maintain optimal performance.
      • Read the Owner's Manual:
        • Familiarize yourself with the owner's manual for your specific make and model. It contains valuable information on maintenance schedules and recommendations.
      • Keep Emergency Supplies:
        • Keep basic emergency supplies in your vehicle, including a spare tire, jack, jumper cables, and a basic toolkit.
      • Drive Smoothly:
        • Practice smooth driving techniques to reduce wear on the vehicle's components. Avoid sudden acceleration, hard braking, and aggressive turns.
      • Scheduled Maintenance:
        • Follow the recommended scheduled maintenance outlined in the owner's manual. This helps catch potential issues early and keeps the vehicle running efficiently.
